About the Workshop

Stock prices of Melon Musk's company Tezla has recently risen after the merger with its once rival, Bapple Inc., as many financial gurus are expecting this deal to be very profitable to investors in the near future.
A dusty old computer somewhere in the large datacenters right next to Wall Street, however, detected unusual buying habits of one of the Bapple's chairperson's trusted advisors, Mr. J.P. Murugan, right before the merger. It seems just two days before the deal was announced, Mr. Murugan bought a hefty stake in Tezla stocks.
Some folks in the financial protection department of the city suspect fraudulent activity and have called upon you to take a look. It's your job now to find out whether indeed this was just a coincidence, or if there's something else lurking in the background.

This in-depth look at data analysis and machine learning in the field of finance is intended to be a ground up and hands-on workshop for commerce students and those interested in how the stock market works. This course will use Python to realise topics covered, but knowledge of programming is not essential to attend. Even though mathematics does form a basis for a field like finance or economics, this workshop will be understandable for even a student who has not taken up mathematics. It will provide more of an intuitive grasp of financial regulation and basic machine learning processes.

Target Audience

The workshop is for students who are interested in commerce subjects, especially stock market regulation and prediction. Some machine learning concepts will be taught as well. You do not need a high level of base knowledge in math and programming, although reading through the references is highly suggested. If you are interested or have doubts regarding the workshop or topics discussed, please email us and we will get back to you as soon as possible.
Attending the Python workshop is suggested but not required.
